Dishes That Define Us

Yam and vegetable stew

Yam and vegetable stew is a Nigerian dish made by boiling cubed yam until soft, then simmering it in a rich sauce of palm oil, leafy greens (like spinach or ugu), crayfish, onions, and peppers. It is a nutritious meal often prepared in one pot, providing a soft texture with savory, leafy flavors.

₦32,000

Rice and prepared stew

Rice and stew is a staple Nigerian dish featuring fluffy white rice paired with a rich, savory tomato-based sauce, often simmered with chicken, beef, or fish. The stew is made by blending tomatoes, peppers, and onions, then frying this mixture in oil with seasonings until the oil floats to the top

₦18,500

Fried Rice

Fried rice is a dish of cooked rice that has been stir-fried in a wok or a frying pan and is usually mixed with other ingredients such as eggs, vegetables, seafood, or meat. It is often eaten as a main dish or as an accompaniment to another dish.

₦28,000

Egusi soap with akpu

Akpu (fermented cassava fufu) and Egusi soup is a popular Nigerian meal, particularly in Igbo cuisine, often enjoyed for its thick, rich texture. The soup is made from ground melon seeds, palm oil, vegetables, and assorted meats, while the akpu provides a soft, fermented, moldable swallow.

₦12,000
